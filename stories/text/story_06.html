The Missouri River joins the Mississippi at St. Louis, forming the world’s fourth longest river system. In 2015, a pipeline burst and spilled about 3 million gallons of saltwater waste into the Missouri River. What is known as one of the worst environmental disasters in North Dakota had direct impacts on the whole Missouri and Mississippi river system. The oil and gas industry is using fracking all over the Bakken formation. In North Dakota, an average of two gallons of wastewater per minute are spilled. We decided to take a closer look at Western North Dakota to visualize the harmful relationship between industrial projects and the environment where humans and non-humans live.
